# Creative brief

## Purpose
A creative brief is the single source of truth for the entire creative team. Every sub-agent reads it before starting work. Write it once, make it precise.

## Format

Post the brief as a comment on the parent issue. Use this exact structure:

```
## Creative brief — [Project name]

**Objective**
What does this campaign need to achieve? One sentence, tied to the company goal.

**Audience**
Who are we talking to? Describe the primary target: demographics, mindset, what they care about, what they fear.

**Single key message**
If the audience remembers only one thing, it should be: [statement].

**Supporting messages** (max 3)
1. [Message A]
2. [Message B]
3. [Message C]

**Tone & voice**
[3 adjectives that describe how we sound]
Not: [1 anti-example — what we must avoid sounding like]

**Visual territory**
[Describe the feeling, not specific designs. Colors, energy, density, references.]

**Deliverables**
List every asset needed with format and owner:
- [Asset] — [Format] — [Assigned to: Agent]

**Timeline**
- Brand territory ready: [relative date or heartbeat]
- Copy + visuals ready: [relative date]
- Final review: [relative date]

**Constraints**
[Budget, brand guidelines, platform restrictions, legal notes]

**Success metric**
How will we know this worked?
```

## Rules
- Every field is mandatory. If you don't have the information, note "TBD — needs input from CEO" and mark the issue blocked.
- The single key message must be one sentence only. If you have two, choose one.
- Tone adjectives must be specific. "Bold, warm, irreverent" is good. "Professional, modern, clean" tells the team nothing.
- Visual territory is not a design brief. Do not specify fonts, hex codes, or layouts here — that is Visual Designer's job. Describe mood and feeling only.
